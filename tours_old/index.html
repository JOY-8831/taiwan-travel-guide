✨ Suggested Tours

Based on your travel style, we recommend these routes:

Northern Coast Exploration

A coastal journey through geological wonders, mountain towns, and lantern culture.

Yehliu Geopark CH: 野柳地質公園
Explore dramatic rock formations and coastal views shaped by the sea and wind. The landmark is the Queen's Head.
➜
Baimiweng Fort CH: 白米甕砲臺
Baimiweng Fort is a historic coastal fort in Keelung built during the Japanese era for harbor defense.
➜
Xian Dong Yan (Fairy Cave) CH: 仙洞巖
A natural sea-eroded cave. Inside, you'll find a temple built during the Qing Dynasty. Free entry.
➜
Jiufen Old Street CH: 九份老街
Originally a gold rush town, now it is renowned for its red lanterns, nostalgic atmosphere, and panoramic ocean views.
➜
Pingxi Old Street CH: 平溪老街
Historical town famous for sky lanterns with wishes and the narrow street with train going through it.

Beitou Hot Spring Walk

Discover hot spring heritage and Japanese-era architecture.

Beitou Library CH: 北投圖書館
A famous eco-friendly wooden library.
➜
Beitou Hot Spring Museum CH: 北投溫泉博物館
This museum is housed in public hot spring bath built during the Japanese colonial era.
➜
Beitou Thermal Valley (Hell Valley) CH: 地熱谷
A surreal natural hot spring with steaming sulfurous waters.
➜
Beitou Public Hot Spring CH: 北投湯屋
Affordable and authentic public open-air thermal baths.

Taipei Art & Imperial Heritage

Experience the museums in Taipei through history and arts, and night markets.

Taipei Expo Farmers Market CH: 圓山農夫市集
Vibrant weekend market at Expo Park offering specialty produce from across Taiwan.
➜
National Palace Museum CH: 國立故宮博物院
A world-class museum that hosts an collection of treasures kept by Emperors ruling from the Forbidden City.
➜
Taipei Fine Arts Museum CH: 台北市立美術館
Showcasing contemporary Taiwanese art in a modern space.
➜
Shilin Night Market CH: 士林夜市
Taipei’s largest night market, famous for street food and games.

Historical Trade Route

Walk through commercial history, temples, and restored heritage streets.

Hsing Tian Kong CH: 行天宮
Popular temple known for its spiritual guidance and fortune-telling.
➜
Dadaocheng (Dihua Street) CH: 大稻埕
A historic merchant district where old-world charm meets modern creativity. Explore traditional herbal shops, Baroque architecture, and vibrant sunset views at the wharf.
➜
Zhongshan Shopping District CH: 中山區商店街/市集
A stylish area filled with designer boutiques, indie brands, and cozy cafés.
➜
Taipei Main Station Underground CH: 台北車站地下街
A massive underground shopping complex.

Wanhua Spiritual Route

Explore Taipei’s oldest district, temples, and medicine culture.

Chiang Kai-shek Memorial Hall CH: 中正紀念堂
The name origined from the 1st president of ROC. This building was to memorize him.
➜
National Taiwan Museum CH: 國立台灣博物館
Taiwan's oldest museum, featuring nature, anthropology, and history.
➜
Presidential Office Building CH: 總統府
The historic seat of government, blending Baroque and modern styles.
➜
Ximending CH: 西門町
Taipei’s youth fashion hub from Japanese period, packed with trendy shops and vibrant nightlife.
➜
Longshan Temple CH: 龍山寺
One of Taiwan’s oldest temples, housing hundred of statues of Buddhist, Taoist, and Confucian deities.
➜
Herb Alley CH: 青草巷
A narrow lane in Wanhua filled with aromatic herbal shops and traditional brews.
➜
Bopiliao Historical Block CH: 剝皮寮歷史街區
Well-preserved Qing-era street offering a glimpse into old Taipei.
➜
Treasure Hill CH: 寶藏巖
A historic hillside settlement turned into an artist village.

Taipei Skyline & Creative Route

A modern Taipei journey featuring skyline views and creative parks.

Elephant Mountain (Xiangshan) CH: 象山
Offers the most famous panoramic view of Taipei 101 and the city.
➜
Taipei 101 CH: 台北101
Iconic landmark hosting luxury brands and a world-class shopping experience.
➜
Sun Yat-sen Memorial Hall CH: 國父紀念館
Memorial hall dedicated to the founder of the ROC.
➜
Yongkang Commercial District CH: 永康街
Famous for mango ice, scallion pancake, other local snacks and shops.
➜
Huashan 1914 Creative Park CH: 華山1914文創園區
Former sake distillery turned into an art and design hub.
➜
Songshan Cultural Park CH: 松山文創園區
A renovated tobacco factory that’s now a hotspot for design markets.

Maokong Tea Mountain

Ride the cable car to scenic tea plantations and mountain temples.

Maokong Gondola CH: 貓空纜車
Scenic cable car ride overlooking tea plantations and the city skyline.
➜
Tea Promotion Center CH: 鐵觀音包種茶研發中心
Learn about the special Oolong tea cultivation in this region and don't forget to try the tea cuisines nearby.
➜
Camphor Tree & Camphor Lake Trail CH: 樟樹樟湖步道
One can easily finish the route in an hour. Following the trail, you’ll see an old-time farmers’ village with all the facilities.
➜
Zhinan Temple CH: 指南宮
Famed Taoist temple on Maokong Mountain with stunning city views.

Yilan Coast & Waterfall Nature

Enjoy surfing beaches, scenic waterfalls, and hot springs.

Wushi Harbor CH: 烏石港
A popular spot for surfing and whale watching. Don't forget the fresh seafood that cooked in Taiwanese way!
➜
Wufengqi Waterfalls CH: 五峰旗瀑布
If you like the mountains more, you can reach this lush forest from the coast in half hour. It is a three-tiered waterfall.
➜
ChiaoHsi Hot spring CH: 礁溪溫泉
Jiaoxi Hot Spring is crystal-clear, odorless, yet contains a considerable amount of minerals like Potassium hydrogen carbonate. Find a hotel or hot spring park to enjoy it!

Tainan Ancient Capital

Travel through Taiwan’s oldest city filled with colonial history.

Chihkan Tower CH: 赤崁樓
Historic landmark and architectural gem from the Dutch and Qing eras.
➜
Tainan Confucius Temple CH: 台南孔廟
The first Confucius temple in Taiwan built in 1665, symbolizing academic tradition.
➜
Fort Zeelandia CH: 安平古堡 (熱蘭遮城)
Former Dutch fortress marking Taiwan’s early modern history.
➜
AnPing Tree House CH: 安平樹屋
It is originally served as the warehouse of Tait& Co. Merchant House. Now it is covered with banyan roots.
➜
Sicao Green Tunnel CH: 四草綠色隧道
You can have a boat ride through a mangrove forest with a Taiwan-style conical hat to avoid sunburn.
➜
Blueprint Cultural Park CH: 藍晒圖文創園區
Famous for its blueprint wall art and indie shops.

Kaohsiung Harbor Art

Explore the southern harbor city blending industry and art.

Lotus Pond (Lianchihtan) CH: 蓮池潭/龍虎塔
It's said that entering at the dragon's mouth and exiting from the tiger's mouth will enjoy good luck.
➜
Ciaotou Sugar Factory CH: 橋頭糖廠
Japanese built this refinery to make taiwan the most advanced sugar producer in the South-east Asia in 1901. The whole factory is open to public.
➜
British Consulate at Takao CH: 打狗英國領事館
Colonial-style building with panoramic harbor views.
➜
Shoushan (Monkey Mountain) CH: 壽山
Home to wild macaques and limestone caves with harbor views. The hotspring here is different from
➜
Pier-2 Art Center CH: 駁二藝術特區
A vibrant arts district by the harbor with murals and creative markets.

Taitung Nature & Indigenous Culture

Experience eastern Taiwan’s pristine nature and traditions.

Taitung Flowing Lake CH: 台東活水湖
Located in Taitung Forest Park, ideal for swimming, canoeing, birdwatching and relaxing your body and mind.
➜
Taitung Tianhou Temple CH: 台東天后宮
A Qing-dynasty temple dedicated to goddess Mazu, who provided spring water to relieve the army.
➜
Yuan Sen Botanical Garden CH: 原生應用植物園
Not just an educational garden showcasing Taiwan’s medicinal plants, but also offer hot pots with various rare herbs.
➜
Taitung Railway Art Village CH: 鐵道藝術村
A creative hub transforming old railway buildings into art studios, shops, and performance spaces.
